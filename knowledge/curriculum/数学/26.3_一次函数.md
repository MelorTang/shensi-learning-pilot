# 一次函数

## Core Idea
The core idea is to introduce the concept of a function, specifically linear functions (一次函数), where a dependent variable changes at a constant rate relative to an independent variable. It establishes the critical connection between algebraic equations $y = kx + b$ and geometric straight lines, emphasizing the concepts of slope (rate of change) and y-intercept.

## Learning Steps
1. **Variables and Functions**: Grasp the definition of variables, constants, and the concept of a function mapping inputs to unique outputs.
2. **Function Graphs**: Learn how to plot functions on a Cartesian coordinate plane.
3. **Direct Proportion Functions (正比例函数)**: Study $y = kx$ ($k \neq 0$), recognizing that its graph is a straight line passing through the origin. Understand the significance of the proportionality constant $k$.
4. **Linear Functions (一次函数)**: Study $y = kx + b$ ($k \neq 0$), its graph, and properties. Understand how $k$ determines the slope/direction and $b$ determines the y-intercept.
5. **Functions, Equations, and Inequalities**: Connect $y = kx + b$ to linear equations in one variable (when $y = 0$) and linear inequalities (when $y > 0$ or $y < 0$).
6. **Applications**: Use linear functions to model and solve real-world problems involving constant rates, such as speed, cost, and pricing.

## Common Pitfalls
- **Meaning of $k$ and $b$**: Failing to deduce the signs of $k$ and $b$ from the quadrants the line passes through, or vice versa.
- **Domain Restrictions**: Forgetting to specify or check the domain of the independent variable in real-world application problems (e.g., time or quantity cannot be negative).
- **Equation vs. Function**: Confusing solving an equation $kx+b=0$ with evaluating the function $y=kx+b$.

## Parent Guidance
- **Discuss Rates of Change**: Relate linear functions to daily life phenomena, such as a taxi fare (base fare $b$ plus rate per mile $k$) or monthly phone bills.
- **Graphing Practice**: Encourage your child to sketch rough graphs rapidly just by looking at the equation. Ask them questions like, "Will this line go up or down? Where does it cross the y-axis?"
- **Step-by-Step Translation**: For word problems, help them translate English sentences into mathematical equations step-by-step, identifying the initial value ($b$) and the rate of change ($k$).

## Linked Mistake Concepts
- **Linear Equations**: Weakness in solving $kx+b=0$ directly impacts the ability to find the x-intercept of a linear function.
- **Inequalities**: Difficulty in interpreting graphs to solve $kx+b > 0$, confusing the x-values for which the y-values are positive.
- **Coordinate System**: Inability to accurately plot points and read coordinates hinders the understanding of function graphs.

## Examples
**Example Problem:**
A car rental company charges a fixed daily fee plus a per-mile charge. Renting a car for one day and driving 50 miles costs $80. Renting the same car for one day and driving 100 miles costs $110. 
1. Find the linear function $y$ (total cost) in terms of $x$ (miles driven).
2. How much would it cost to rent the car for one day and drive 150 miles?

**Solution:**
```text
1. Let the linear function be y = kx + b, where k is the per-mile charge and b is the fixed daily fee.
Based on the problem, we have two points: (50, 80) and (100, 110).
Substitute these into the equation to form a system of linear equations:
80 = 50k + b
110 = 100k + b

Subtract the first equation from the second:
30 = 50k
k = 30 / 50 = 0.6

Substitute k = 0.6 back into the first equation:
80 = 50(0.6) + b
80 = 30 + b
b = 50

The linear function is y = 0.6x + 50.

2. To find the cost for 150 miles, substitute x = 150 into the function:
y = 0.6(150) + 50
y = 90 + 50 = 140

It would cost $140 to rent the car for one day and drive 150 miles.
```
